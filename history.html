History of Manchester United

Manchester United was founded in 1878 under the name Newton Heath LYR Football Club. The club was formed by workers from the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway depot.

In 1902, the club changed its name to Manchester United after facing financial difficulties. New ownership helped rebuild the club and began a new era in its history.

Manchester United plays its home matches at Old Trafford, one of the most famous football stadiums in the world. The stadium has been the home of the club since 1910 and is often called the "Theatre of Dreams."

Throughout its history, Manchester United has been known for attacking football, developing young players, and achieving success in English and European competitions.
